Quanjing Chen

PhD in Brain and Cognitive Sciences

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at University of Rochester Medical Center, NY. I work in the Cog T Lab with Dr. Vankee Lin. My CV is here. Contact: quanjingchen (AT) gmail

In 2018 I completed my PhD work under the supervision of Dr. Brad Mahon in the Department of Brain and Cognitive Sciences at the University of Rochester. I used patterned based fMRI to decode how information about manipulable objects is organized in the human brain with machine learning techniques.

In my postdoctoral work I use functional and structural MRI, functional connectivity, diffusion MRI, psychophysiology and cognitive neuropsychological methods. By applying machine learning and deep learning to brain data, I'm trying to find sensitive biomarkers for early detection of Alzheimer's disease (AD) associated neurodegeneration and develop cognitive interventions that promote cognitive health.

In my spare time, I play the piano, go running and skating, read books and watch movies and TV series. I also enjoy hanging out with my friends, finding new restaurants, and catching up on sleep.
